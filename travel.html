Traveling to Cancun

Cancún, a Mexican city on the Yucatán Peninsula bordering the Caribbean Sea, is known for its beaches, numerous resorts and energetic nightlife. It's composed of 2 distinct areas: the more traditional downtown area, El Centro, and Zona Hotelera, a long, beachfront strip of high-rise hotels, nightclubs, shops and restaurants.

In early 2010, there was a major beach restoration project that included the rebuilding of the beaches in Cancun, Playa del Carmen, and Cozumel. The Mexican government invested $71 million US Dollars in the project, which restored 11 kilometers of the area's beaches. Cancun's beaches have white sand, which is made from crushed coral and is not hot to the touch

Weather

The city is warm year-round, and moderated by onshore trade winds - helping Cancun's average annual temperature rise to a comfortable 80 degrees. There are over 240 sunny days throughout the year. Some of the best weather you will ever see!

  • Cancun International Airport & Customs

    Cancun International Airport handles the second largest volume of airport traffic in Mexico. With its new terminal it now receives extremely high scores for its modern facilities and equipment, and above all for its strict security level. The airport complies with all US FAA specifications.

  • Customs Procedures at Cancun Airport

    During the inbound flight, you will receive and need to fill out a customs form (Hacienda). After passing through immigration, you collect your bags and proceed to the exit. You will hand the form to the customs agent and press the button on what looks like a traffic light. A green light means "go" and red means "inspection" in which case, officials perform a quick inspection.

Hotel & Resort

Live Aqua Cancún invites you to "Be Yourself" at its adults-only resort situated in the middle of the Hotel Zone. And it certainly does not lack personalized touches.

Live Aqua's seven pools, including infinity and lap pools, come in varying temperatures, so one of them is sure to be just right. You'll also have access to the beach, where you can post up in a private cabana. And as far as dining goes, you'll have your pick of 12 dining spots and bars, which serve up everything from

Mediterranean fare to traditional Mexican cuisine. When you're ready to turn in for the evening, you'll find an aromatherapy kit, along with a plasma TV and a spacious bathtub and shower in your suite.

Another bonus: Previous guests say the vistas overlooking the Caribbean Seas from the ocean-facing rooms are impressive. Past visitors also praise the gracious hotel staff, who they say went above and beyond to cater to guests' needs.
